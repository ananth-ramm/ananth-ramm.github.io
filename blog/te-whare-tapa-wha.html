Te Whare Tapa Wha


Te Whare Tapa Whā is a model of the 4 dimensions of wellbeing developed by Tā Mason Durie in 1984 to provide a Māori perspective on health.

Taha Tinana - Physical Wellbeing


  • Gymming eveyday/most days 2-3 hours resistance training

  • One indoor exercise and one outdoor such as running, basketball, aerobic training


taha hinengaro - mental wellbeing


  • Gym usually helps

  • Practicing my mindfulness and meditation

  • Maintaining a balanced diet

  • Maintaining a consistent sleep pattern


taha wairua - spiritual wellbeing



  • Trying to rebuild my relationship with God

  • Making praying a routine and priority


Taha Whānau - Family Wellbeing
  • Try to sit at the dinner table and enjoy a meal with my family more often

  • Try to connect more maybe through board games or indoor/outdoor activities
Whenua - Interconnections to the land and environment
  • Going out for walk in the sun and embrace nature

  • Try go to the beach when I can
